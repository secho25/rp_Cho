CNN-Based Predictive Temperature Modeling for Urban Heat Island Mitigation Planning

Research Practicum 2024-2025

Background

The urban heat island (UHI) effect is a phenomenon in which the temperature of cities is significantly higher than that of their rural surroundings. Urbanization and climate change have intensified the UHI effect in recent years, exacerbating economic, social, and health problems in urban areas.

Rationale

Although mitigation of UHIs has grown increasingly critical, current approaches primarily focus on analyzing existing conditions rather than predicting the thermal impacts of potential interventions, limiting their applicability in informing urban planning decisions. This study therefore develops a convolutional neural network that enables predictive intervention scenarios by allowing urban planners to adjust parameters such as vegetation, built-up area, and urban density indices to forecast and quantify temperature changes caused by proposed mitigation strategies.

Research Practicum

Research Practicum is a course that all students at High Technology High School are required to take. Students work to design and develop a research project of nearly any topic of interest in freshman year in the Introduction to Research course, then pursue their experiment of choice in sophomore year. A final research paper must be written by January.
